# Stakeholder Map — Human Clarity Framework  
TOGAF Phase A — Architecture Vision

This Stakeholder Map identifies the key stakeholders of the Human Clarity Framework, their concerns, influence, interest, and the viewpoints required to address their needs.  
It ensures the architecture aligns with real human, social, and organizational expectations.

---

# 1. Stakeholder List

## Primary Stakeholders
### 1. Individuals Seeking Clarity
**Role:** End users  
**Concerns:**  
- Emotional stability  
- Reducing confusion  
- Understanding patterns  
- Making better decisions  
**Influence:** Medium  
**Interest:** High  

### 2. Counselors / Coaches (Non‑Clinical)
**Role:** Facilitators using the framework  
**Concerns:**  
- Practical tools

- Ease of teaching  
**Influence:** High  
**Interest:** High  

### 3. Community Centers / Nonprofits
**Role:** Program implementers  
**Concerns:**

- Low‑cost adoption

**Influence:** Medium  
**Interest:** Medium  

### 4. Schools / Youth Programs
**Role:** Early‑stage clarity educators  
**Concerns:**

**Role:** Organizational adopters

**Influence:** High

# 6. Engagement Strategy Summary

- **High Influence + High Interest:**  
  Co‑create models, gather feedback, involve in governance.

- **High Influence + Low Interest:**  
  Provide concise summaries and ensure alignment with their goals.

- **Low Influence + High Interest:**  
  Provide clear, simple tools and educational materials.

- **Low Influence + Low Interest:**  
  Monitor lightly; engage only when needed.

# Notes
- This Stakeholder Map follows TOGAF’s recommended structure: stakeholder → concerns → influence/interest → engagement.  
- It directly supports the Architecture Vision and guides all future architecture views.  
- It ensures the Human Clarity Framework remains safe, ethical, and aligned with real human needs.
