# Architecture Principles — Human Clarity Framework

Architecture principles define the fundamental rules and guidelines that shape the Human Clarity Framework.  
They ensure consistency, integrity, and long-term alignment across all models, capabilities, and reference architectures.

---

## 1. Clarity First
**Statement:** Every model, pattern, or artifact must reduce confusion, not increase it.  
**Rationale:** Human suffering often comes from unclear emotions, unclear decisions, and unclear patterns.  
**Implications:**  
- Models must be simple and intuitive  
- No jargon unless absolutely necessary  
- Visual clarity is prioritized over complexity  

---

## 2. Emotional Safety
**Statement:** All artifacts must support emotional wellbeing and avoid harm.  
**Rationale:** This framework is non-clinical and must never trigger distress or judgment.  
**Implications:**  
- No diagnostic language  
- No labeling of people  
- All models must be supportive and neutral  

---

## 3. Pattern Transparency
**Statement:** Human patterns must be visible, explainable, and traceable.  
**Rationale:** People repeat behaviors they cannot see; transparency enables change.  
**Implications:**  
- Models must highlight triggers, loops, and consequences  
- Pattern maps must be easy to interpret  
- No hidden logic or black-box reasoning  

---

## 4. Decision Integrity
**Statement:** Decisions should align with long-term wellbeing and personal values.  
**Rationale:** Most life problems come from reactive or unclear decisions.  
**Implications:**  
- Decision models must show trade-offs  
- Long-term vs short-term clarity must be explicit  
- Decision templates must include value alignment  

---

## 5. Simplicity Over Complexity
**Statement:** The simplest model that works is the correct model.  
**Rationale:** Complexity reduces adoption and increases confusion.  
**Implications:**  
- Avoid over-modeling  
- Use minimal layers unless needed  
- Prioritize usability over theoretical completeness  

---

## 6. Universality
**Statement:** The framework must work across cultures, ages, and backgrounds.  
**Rationale:** Human clarity is universal; the architecture must be inclusive.  
**Implications:**  
- Avoid culturally specific assumptions  
- Use neutral language  
- Ensure models apply broadly  

---

## 7. Modularity
**Statement:** Each component of the framework must stand alone and integrate cleanly.  
**Rationale:** Users may adopt only parts of the framework.  
**Implications:**  
- Capabilities, models, and patterns must be modular  
- No dependency on a single master diagram  
- Each artifact must have a clear boundary  

---

## 8. Traceability
**Statement:** Every model must trace back to a driver, goal, or requirement.  
**Rationale:** Ensures architectural integrity and prevents random additions.  
**Implications:**  
- Motivation View is the source of truth  
- All artifacts must reference goals or outcomes  
- No orphan diagrams  

---

## Notes
These principles follow TOGAF’s structure: Statement, Rationale, and Implications.  
They guide all future artifacts including capability maps, reference architectures, and ArchiMate models.
